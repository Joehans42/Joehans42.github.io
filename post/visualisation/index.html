Interactive Visualisation

December 5, 2021

Pyvis

To visualise the data, I have used the Python framework, Pyvis. It is an interactive visualisation that allows for zooming in on individual nodes and their edges. What you see on the graph below here is a sample of the entire SNAP data set. Since Python has a hard time plotting and visualising 4604 nodes with 119882 edges, I have simply sampled 2000 edges from the network and plotted them with Pyvis. One of the consequences of this is of course, that we don’t get a sense of how fully connected the real network actually is, and there are no nodes with a degree of 0. We learned about those things from the analysis, but looking at the plot we see that, while there is a giant connected component with a majority of the nodes, there are actually quite a few nodes outside of the GCC connected to only a few other individual nodes. We know that this is not the case in the full network.
